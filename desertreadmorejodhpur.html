MEHRANGARH FORT

Catching a bird’s eye view of the ‘Blue City’ from the top of the fort is a sight to behold. To watch the houses closely, you can delve deep into the meandering markets jam-packed around the Clock Tower. Explore the colossal fort and the impressive in-house museum to experience the grandeur of bygone eras. Plan your visit in the evening hours to watch this Citadel of the Sun in all its resplendent glory. With the crimson sky bathing everything in golden hues and the blue houses lending the desert landscape a pop of lively color, this hill-fort standing 400 feet above the ground offers one of the finest spectacles that you will come across in Rajasthan.
Timings: 9:00 am to 5:00 pm
Fort Entry Fee: Free
Museum Entry Fee: ₹ 50 to ₹ 100 for Indians
₹ 600 for foreigners


ENJOY A SESSION OF ZIP LINING WITH JODHPUR FLYING FOX

Imagine gliding over the majestic Mehrangarh Fort or flying past two desert lakes to arrive at the Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park from where you can enjoy incredible views of the fort as well as the city. Sounds exciting? Go for a session of zip lining with Jodhpur Flying Fox, which is one of the absolutely fun things to do in Jodhpur.
Location: Mehrangarh Fort
Timings: 9:00 am to 5:00 pm
Duration: 45 to 90 minutes
Cost: ₹ 1400 onwards, depending on the session selected


ENJOY SOME TRANQUIL MOMENTS AT JASWANT THADA

Jaswant Thada is an intricately crafted marble cenotaph built by Maharaja Sardar Singh in 1899 to commemorate his father, Maharaja Jaswant Singh II. The remarkable memorial features a tiered garden and impressive gazebos. Spend some time at this serene place from where you can enjoy stunning views of the Mehrangarh Fort and the city.
Location: Near Mehrangarh Fort
Timings: 9:00 am to 5:00 pm
Entry Fee: Enjoy Some Tranquil Moments at Jaswant Thada ₹ 30 for Indian; ₹ 50 for foreigners


RAPPEL THROUGH THE TALLEST ROCKS IN SOORSAGAR

If you are a thrill seeker, one of the best things to do in Jodhpur is to go on a rappelling adventure in Soorsagar. This adventurous hotspot is just 13 km away from Jodhpur and is dotted with several big and small rocks, which provides the perfect setting for rappelling. With rocks as tall as 110 feet, get ready for a rush of adrenaline as you rappel down the rocky surfaces.
Duration: 1 hour (approx.)
Cost: ₹ 500 onwards


VISIT UMAID BHAWAN PALACE

Did you know that the Umaid Bhawan Palace is the last royal palace built in India before the country gained independence? Currently, the palace functions as a royal residence, a heritage luxury hotel, and a family museum, which is the only part accessible to the general public. The museum houses a fascinating collection of artifacts that depict the history of the Jodhpur royal family.
Timings: 10:00 am to 5:00 pm; closed on Sundays
Entry Fee: ₹ 30 for adults; ₹ 10 for kids; ₹ 100 for foreigners . STROLL THROUGH THE MANDORE GARDENS

The ancient town of Mandore was once the capital of the Marwar region and is one of the top tourist places to visit in Jodhpur. Visit the Mandore Gardens to get a glimpse of its glorious past and to click some stunning pictures. The garden comprises age-old temples, high-rock terraces, and memorials dedicated to the Maharajas of Jodhpur. Among the highlights of the place are the Hall of Fame, the Temple of 33 crore Gods, and the Government Museum.
Location: Mandore
Timings: 9:00 am to 8:00 pm
Entry fee: Free; ₹ 50 for museum entry


ENJOY A CANDLE-LIT ROOFTOP DINNER AT DARIKHANA

How about enjoying a romantic dinner on a candle-lit rooftop restaurant with your SO, with mesmerizing views of the Mehrangarh Fort and sumptuous food and drinks adding to the experience? No wonder, dining at Darikhana often features among the list of things to do in Jodhpur for couples. Note that prior reservation is required at this restaurant.
Location: Kabir Nagar
Timings: 11:00 am to 11:00 pm
Cost for Two: ₹ 3000 approx.


PAY YOUR RESPECT AT CHAMUNDA MATA TEMPLE

Chamunda Mata Temple holds much religious significance for the people of Jodhpur. Located on the southern part of the Mehrangarh Fort, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Chamunda, the main deity of the Jodhpur royal family. Visit the ancient temple to feel the spiritual vibe of the place and to seek blessings from the black stone idol of the goddess.
Location: Gulab Sagar
Timings: 5:00 am to 12:00 pm and 4:00 pm to 9:00 pm


TASTE JODHPUR'S STREET FOOD

Rajasthan’s culinary culture is legendary. And the streets Jodhpur offer you mouth-watering delicacies that celebrate the region’s gastronomic legacy. Here is where you can sample some of the best street food in Jodhpur:
Mirchi Vada and Mawa Kachori (Janta Sweet Home, Nai Sarak)
Makhani lassi (Mishrilal Hotel, Ghantaghar Market)
Omelette (Outside the northern exit of Sardar Market)
Daal Baati Choorma (Bhavani Dal Bati, Sardarpura)
Samosa (Shahi Samosa, Nai Sarak, Clock Tower area)
Masala Chai (Bhatti Tea Stall, Sardarpura)


RELIEVE HISTORY AT SARDAR GOVERNMENT MUSEUM

A visit to Sardar Government Museum counts among the top things to do in Jodhpur for history buffs. Built by Maharaja Umaid Singh, this museum is located within the premises of Umaid Garden, a public park in the city. The major exhibits here include various collections of weapons, textiles, portraits and miniature paintings of the erstwhile kings, metallic artifacts, stuffed animals, and much more.
Location: Sri Ganganagar
Location: Sri Ganganagar
Timings: 10:00 am to 5:00 pm; Closed on Fridays and Public Holidays
