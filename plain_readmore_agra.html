WITNESS THE CHARM OF TAJ MAHAL

The massive ivory-white structure” renowned as the symbol of unconditional love, was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in the loving memory of his third wife Mumtaz Mahal. This mausoleum, is one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Often called the “Jewel of Muslim artistry”, this monument is a frequented tourist spot by people, from all the vertices of the earth. Laced with beautiful lush green gardens and, scintillating fountains the beauty of the edifice is miraculous. Marking its significance in history, this monument was crafted by more than 20,000 artisans and architects exhibit excellent skill-set of the Mughal architectural style.
Timings: The site can be visited from 6:00 am to 6:30 pm on all days except Fridays.
Charges: The entry fee is Rs 40 for Indian and Rs 1000 for foreigners.


EXPLORE THE JEWEL OF Agra- AGRA FORT

With rich historical background, this majestic fort marks its place in most-visited tourist place in Agra. Sprawling over an area of 94 acres, this fort has four gates namely the Khizri Gate, Delhi, Lahore Gate and, Amar Singh Gate. The mighty structure displays, elegant artistry of Hindu and, Mughal styles and, visiting this fort is one of the best things to do in Agra.
Timings: One can visit from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm on all days.
Charges: The entry fee is INR 40 for Indian tourist and INR 550 for foreign tourist.


EXPLORE THE SANDSTONE CITY- FATEHPUR SIKRI

Renowned as “The City Of Victory”, it was founded by the Mughal emperor, Akbar. Exhibiting a fusion of Hindu, Jain and Islamic style of architecture, this city is built using the Red sandstone also called as the “Sikri Sandstone”. With 9 gates namely, Chandan Pal Gate, Agra Gate, Tehra Gate, Lal Gate, Delhi Gate, Birbal Gate, Gwalior Gate, Ajmeri Gate, and Chor Gate, one can enter the city. The region hems in various sites, built on Indian architectural principles like royal palaces, private quarters, harems, different utility buildings, court, and mosques.
Timings: Open daily from sunrise to sunset.
Charges: The entry fee of Indian tourist is INR 50 and INR 485 for foreign tourist.


DELVE INTO THE BEAUTY OF JAHANGIR MAHAL

Beautifully built Palace for Akbar’s wife Jahangir Mahal, is one of the remarkable places in Agra. Showcasing elegant Islamic architecture, one can take a glance at the exceptional artistry. The major source of attraction of this palace, is the huge bowl carved out of a rock, called the “Hauz-i-Jahangiri”, which was used as the container for fragrant rose water. The majestic palace has extraordinary carvings which will blow off your mind. Visit here and, fall in love with the medieval architectural style.
Timings: One can visit the Jahangir Mahal on all days from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm
Charges:The fee is INR 5 for Indian tourist and INR 30 for foreign tourist.


DELVE INTO THE SPIRITUALITY OF JAMA MASJID

One of the largest mosques in India “The Jama Masjid” is also famed as the, “Friday Mosque”. Beautifully arched gate supported by pillars will bind you in its charm. Built by Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, who dedicated it to his adorable daughter Jahanara Begum exhibits his love for his daughter. Visiting Jama Masjid is one of the best things to do in Agra.
Timings: Open on all days from sunrise to sunset.
Charges: There is no entry fee to enter the premises.


MEHTAB BAGH

Located at the banks of the Yamuna, this splendid garden offers phenomenal displays lying opposite to, the Taj Mahal. Sprawling over 25 acres, this glorious bagh marks its place in best gardens of Agra. While you walk through this enchanting garden, you will breathe in the fresh fragrance of various species of flowers. The garden brims over with fountains, trees, and beautiful pools, which make it a picture perfect place.
Timings: One can visit Mehtab Bagh from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm on all days.
Charges: The entry fee is INR 15 for adults and INR 200 for foreigners.


EXPLORE THE CHARMING GARDEN-ANGURI BAGH

This elegantly built garden facing the Khas Mahal, owns small hexagonal grids of red sandstone, designed in a particular pattern. Surrounded with double-storey sandstone buildings, covered by white-limestone offers spectacular displays.This Bagh is a historical gem of Agra and is frequented by locals as well as tourists.
Timings: One can visit this from 6:00 am in the morning to 7:30 pm in the evening on all days.
Charges: The entry fee is INR 20 for Indian tourist and INR 300 for a foreigner.


UNRAVEL, THE CHARM OF DAYAL BAGH

With the literal meaning “ Garden of the Merciful”, this wonderful garden attracts masses from near and far. Located adjacent to the Soami Bagh, this site holds the samadhi of "Swamiji Maharaj”, who founded Radha Swami. Built with pure white marble, this elegant structure exhibits excellent pictures and, fine pietra dura inlay work on the inner walls. This site has high spiritual as well as historical importance.
Timings: One can visit this place from 6:00 am to 6:30 pm on all days.
Charges: There is no entry fee to visit Dayal Bagh.


WITNESS THE SERENITY OF HUMAYUN'S MOSQUE

One of the oldest monuments to be built in the Mughal dynasty “Humayun’s Mosque”, has Persian inscriptions carved on it. This spectacular artistry of the Mughal period, was used named “Kabul” by the locals and, catering to the religious needs of the Mughals. The serenity is interweaved in the aura of the structure and, is built entirely out of brick masonry. Visiting this sacred placed is one of the must-do, in the list of things to do in Agra.
Timings: The site is open from 6:00 am to 7:30 pm
Charges: The entry fee is INR 30 for Indian tourist and INR 500 for foreigners.


WITNESS THE HISTORICALLY RICH-ITIMAD-UD-DULAH

Built by Nur Jahan, wife of the Mughal Emperor Jahangir, this magnificent edifice is located on the eastern bank of Yamuna. Established in memory of her father Mirza Ghiyas Beg, who was titled as the Itimad-Ud-Daulah, which means “Pillar of the Government. Commonly known as the Baby Taj as it resembles the Iconic “Taj Mahal”. The structure is embellished with semi-precious stones and, is arched with greenery all over. Visiting Itimad-Ud-Daulah is one of the best things to do in Agra, as it is less crowded in comparison to other sites
Timings:The site is open from sunrise to sunset on all days.
Charges: The entry fee is INR 10 for Indian tourist and INR 110 for foreign tourist.


A PERFECT PLACE FOR A SHOPAHOLIC-KINARI BAZAR

“Buzzing Market”, the Kinari Bazaar, is well-stocked with all the essentials and traditional stuff. Undoubtedly a perfect place for a shopaholic, as here you can find A to Z items like lehengas, dupattas, salwar kameez, kurtas, sherwanis and what not. As you enter the market you will get awestruck by the colorful outfits, beautifully laced with gold and silver. You will always end up with a bag full of goods as this market will never disappoint you with its charm.
Timings: The market usually open at 11:00 am and closes at 9:00 pm on all days except Sunday.
Charges: There are no charges to enter the market.


WITNESS THE MUGHAL ERA AT KALAKRITI DANCE DRAMA SHOW

One of the best things to do in Agra is watching the Kalakriti Dance Drama show, Mohabbat The Taj. The exceptional dramatics by the artisans will take you back to the Mughal era, through their vigorous performance. This power-packed performance of 80 artisans, accompanied by the advanced technology gives the lively appearance and, lasts for about 1 hour and 20 minutes. Here, you can choose from various seat options namely silver, gold and, platinum classes.
Timings: The opening hours are 10:00 am to 8:15 pm from Monday to Saturday and 10:30 am to 9:00 pm on Sunday.
Charges: One can book from several classes ranging from INR 1200 to INR 4000.


SAVOR THE LIP-SMACKING DELICACIES

One of the most delightful things to do in Agra, is to savor the mouth-water delicacies of the city. Talking about Agra, the first thing that comes in mind is the “ Exotic Mughlai Cuisine”, which has variants of chicken and, various other delectable dishes. Give your taste buds a feast, at fantastic restaurants in the city and taste the tinge of delicious chaats at Sadar Bazaar . Be it the tandoori chicken, or crispy kebabs here you can have it all. Not only this, but you can also have various other cuisines like Chinese, Continental and, lot more.
Timings: The markets are usually open from 11:00 am to 10:00 pm where you can savor amazing cuisine.


WANDER THROUGH THE STREETS OF AGRA

With numerous places to shop from, Agra has plenty of markets namely, Sadar Bazaar, Subhash Bazaar, Kinari Bazaar, TDI Mall, Shah Market and many more. Here you can find elegant marble crafts, leather goods, handicrafts, sweets, ornaments, religious item and what not. One can also spot several miniature replicas of Taj Mahal, which can be bought as a souvenir of the trip. These markets are always buzzing with flocks of people, indulged in exploring the markets.
Timings: The markets remain open from 11:00 am to 9:00 pm.
Charges: Pay only for what you shop as there are no charges for entering the market.
