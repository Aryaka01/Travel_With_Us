TAKE A TOUR OF MEJESTIC JUNAGARH FORT

Visiting the famous Junagarh Fort is certainly the best experience to have on your Bikaner sightseeing tour. Initially called Chintamani Mahal, this old fort has impeccable architecture, which is so marvellous that it’d leave you spellbound. So, make sure you do not skip this one off your list.
Timings: Open on all the days from 10:00a.m. to 4:30p.m.
Entry Fees: INR 50 for Indian tourists and INR 300 for foreigners
Location: Junagarh Fort Road, Bikaner, Rajasthan


VISIT THE POPULAR KARNI MATA TEMPLE

Home to more than 25,000 rats, this Hindu temple is one of the very unique places to visit in Bikaner. Despite so many rats or ‘kabbas’ around, this place is considered very holy by the locals and makes for an intriguing sight for both locals and tourists alike. So, if you’re in the city, you ought to travel all the way to this attraction.
Timings: Open on all the days from 4:30 AM to 10:00 PM
Entry Fees: There is no entry fee for visiting this temple.
Location: Karni Mata Niji Pranyas, Deshnok, Bikaner, Rajasthan


WITNESS THE SPLENDOUR OF LALLGARH PALACE

Of all the top things to do in Bikaner, visiting the Lalgarh Palace is an absolute must-do. The finest architectural wonder in the city, this palace was built by the then Maharaja of Bikaner. You can not only witness the royalty and history here, but also experience the same by staying at its heritage hotel. Now, that’s something even you wouldn’t want to skip, right?
Timings: Open on all the days from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM, except every Sunday
Entry Fees: INR 10 for Indian tourists, and INR 20 for foreigners
Location: NH15, Samta Nagar, Bikaner, Rajasthan


GO WILDLIFE SPOTTING IN THE GAJNER WILDLIFE SANCTUARY

In Bikaner, things to do are endless, but the best ones are few, of which going wildlife spotting at the famous Gajner Wildlife Sanctuary is a must. With innumerable flora and fauna, this ultimate spot situated in the desert is perfect to visit for calming the wildlife and photography enthusiast within.
Timings: 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM
Entry Fees: INR 100 per person
Location: Gajner, Rajasthan


BIKANER CAMEL FESTIVAL

A two day event organized every year in the month of January, the Camel festival has some spectacular and unusual camel performances like camel races, camel dances, and the bumpy, neck shaking camel rides take place at this event. Among all other havelis like Rikhji Bagri ki Haveli, Haveli of Bhairondan, Daga Chowk Havelis and Sampatlala Agarwal Haveli, Rampuria group of Havelis are known for their more than 400 years old architecture splendour. With intricate details of jharokhas and carvings, havelis are no less than palaces.


NATIONAL RESEARCH CENTRE ON CAMEL

Identified as one of the important tourist place of Bikaner, National Research Centre On Camel is open for tourists during afternoon. Tourists can see the camel of different breeds and their behavior.


VISIT THE MAJESTIC LAXMI NIWAS PALACE

Once a royal palace of Maharaja Ganga Singh, Laxmi Niwas Palace is a stunning attraction which is now open to the public as a heritage hotel. The architectural beauty of the palace and its rich historic and heritage value draw guests in large numbers.


RELISH THE TASTE OF THE LOCAL CUISINES

Like most of the tourists, ‘what to do in Bikaner’ may become one of your concerns too. But believe us, there is so much to see and do around in the city that you’d rather yearn to stay there longer. Likewise, exploring and eating the authentic local delicacies from the best restaurants in Bikaner is a treat you must give to your taste buds.
Popular cuisines: Kachori, dal bati churma, khata, gatta ki sabzi, pakodi, raj bhog, ghevar, fini, and more
Best places to eat: Heeralal’s, Gallops, Chappan Bhog, and more.


CITY SHOPPING

Streets of Bikaner are a shopper's delight! From traditional clothes and footwear to handicrafts and eateries - all are available in the locals Bazaars of Bikaner.
