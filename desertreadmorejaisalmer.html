JAISALMER FORT

It is famously known as Golden Fort of Rajasthan. It was constructed by Raja Jaisal, Rajput ruler in the year 1156 AD. It ranks amongst the largest forts in the world. The golden and yellow mixed sandstone offer magical look to the Fort.
Tickets Charges: 50 per person
Fort Timings: 9:00a.m. – 5:00p.m.(open all days)



BADA BAGH

It means ‘Huge Garden’ which offers stunning views. Maharawal Jait Singh’s period saw its construction, which was in the beginning of 16th century. His son Lunakaran completed the construction after the death of his father. The garden served as a memorial where the nobles and their families were cremated.
Ticket charges: 100 per person
Camera Fee: 100
Timings: 8:00 a.m. to 6:00p.m.(open all days)



PATWON-KI-HAVELI

It is located in the main city. It was built by a famous trader named Guman Chand and his sons. This massive five-storied construction has five intricately decorated huge suites. The large corridors and the decorated walls are excellent representations of the art form that prevailed. The entire construction is made of yellow sandstone. The grandeur and the architecture of the monument add immense value to the cultural heritage of the city. Out of the five havelis, one is converted into a museum. The third haveli houses the works of local craftsmen of the bygone era.
Ticket charges: For Indians-Rs 20 per person
For foreigners-Rs 100 per person
Camera Photograph Fee: Rs 50
Video Camera Fee: Rs 100
Timings: 9:00 a.m. to 5 p.m.(open all days)

SAM SAND DUNES

To have a feel of the desert, you need to undertake Desert Safari. Sam Sand Dunes is the ideal location to set off for the Desert Safari. You will love the safari on camelback that would take you along crests and troughs and you could watch the picturesque sunset on the desert. Jeep safaris are available as well. Desert festival is organized in the months of February and March. If you are around this time, you would not want to miss the folk dances, camel races and other cultural events conducted here.

THAR HERITAGE MUSEUM

Thar Heritage Museum is located at the main market of the city. Laxmi Narayan Khatri was the founder of this museum. The museum presents one the history culture, art and architecture of the famous Thar Desert. Sea fossils aging over million years let us in on how the sea eventually became the Thar Desert. The museum houses unique ornaments of the ship of the desert and horses. Documents and coins of Jaisalmer, ancient manuscripts and weapons are some of the collections found in this museum.
Timings: 9:00a.m. - 8:00p.m.
Time Required: 1-2 Hrs
Entry Fee: Indian: 30 per person
                  Foreigners: 70 per person

GADISAR LAKE

It is located a kilometer away from the fort and was constructed by the first ruler of the land, Raja Rawal Jaisal and later reconstructed by Maharawal Garsi in the year 1367 AD. The people of the desert city depended on this tank for their water needs. You will find temples, Holy shrines and Chattris around the banks of around the banks of this lake. The artistically carved entrance made of yellow sandstone offers breathtaking view.
Opening Hours: 7:00 am to 7:00 pm
Ticket Charges: Rs 200 for 2-seater pedal boat
                            Rs 200 for 4-seater pedal boat
                            Rs 300 for 6 seater rowing boats


KHURI

A small village famous for its sand dunes, Khuri has scenic sunset viewpoints and offers a good look in Thar’s ecosystem due to its location. This is an affordable substitute to Sam Sand dunes and would be ideal for people looking forward to relaxing among lesser crowds. Camel riding, Desert camping are some of the activities that can be done here.


JAISALMER WAX MUSEUM

Fan of history and tales from the past? You might find the Jaisalmer War Museum an interesting place to spend time when visiting Jaisalmer. From stories of battles fought to weapons from the past era, you can learn a lot about these things at the Jaisalmer War Museum.
Ticket Charges: 30 per person
Timings: 9:00 a.m. to 6p.m.


MAHARAJA'S PALACE

The massive palace spells elegance and enchantment as it is located in the Jaisalmer Fort. You can see the palace from any part of the city, it is that huge and gorgeous. Plus, with the golden hues that encapsulate the palatial grounds, the Maharaja's Palace cannot be missed during the day or in the evening.
Ticket Charges: 100 per person
Timings: 9:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.


AMAR SINGH PALACE

Located adjacent to the illustrious Amar Singh Palace, Amar Sagar Lake is a major tourist attraction in Jaisalmer. As the city is predominantly surrounded by desert, the scenic water body becomes one of the major highlights of Jaisalmer. Carry bottled water and snacks.
Carry Sun-glasses and hats to avoid direct sunlight during day visit.
Timings: 9:00a.m. to 5:00p.m.
Time Required: 1-2 Hrs
Entry Fee: No entry fee
